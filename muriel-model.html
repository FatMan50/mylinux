The Muriel Model

This two-story home was designed in the French Country style. It is home that will be great for a growing family. The open floor plan is welcoming while at the same time it has plenty of private spaces.

Having the master suite on the first floor is a “must have” for many people. This design dedicates the left side of the home to master suite. You will enjoy the seclusion of the large bedroom and the luxurious master bath.

Sometime you just want to soak in a warm tub, and you can do that. The master bath has a large soaking tube for when you just need some “me time”. The rest of the time you will be jumping in and out of the large walk-in shower. The double vanity finishes off this master bath that is perfect for the busy couple.

When it is time to lounge around with the family the great room is perfect. The open floor plan makes sure the people in the kitchen are not left out of the fun. The large kitchen island is a great place for quick meals and it is big enough to fit the whole family.

This plan really connects the outdoors with the indoors. The great room has a wall of pocket glass doors that makes the covered rear porch feel like part of the same space. The outdoor kitchen is a summertime attraction for the whole family. Not to mention it is great for entertaining.

You will find the utility room perfectly located between the 2-car garage and the kitchen. The pantry is just around the corner from the utility room. The flow from the kitchen, past the pantry to the dining area is designed to make entertaining effortless.

The study on the first floor can be used as a 5ht bed room. This is thanks to it’s connection to a full bath. The other three bedrooms are located not he second floor. Two of the bedrooms share a bath. But the other bedroom has it’s own private bath. We like to think of this as the second master. It is perfect for an older child or even a mother-in-law apartment.

Exterior features set this home apart. The dual pitched hip roof and the wide porch enhance it’s curb appeal. Decorative architectural appointments such as shutters, flower boxes and iron railings define the French Country look and feel.
